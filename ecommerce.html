E-commerce Platform - Case Study

Improving product discovery and checkout experience for modern online shoppers.

Project Overview

Redesign an e-commerce platform to make product discovery easier and improve the overall shopping flow, including filtering, product exploration, and checkout.

Problem Statement

Users were struggling with slow filtering systems and a cluttered checkout experience, resulting in high cart abandonment rates.

Objective

Create a fast, modern, and responsive e-commerce website that simplifies browsing, product selection, and payment flow.

Target Users

  • Millennials and Gen Z browsing fashion, electronics, and lifestyle products.
  • Busy online shoppers who prefer fast, mobile-first checkout experiences.

Process

  • Research: Conducted surveys and competitor analysis of Zara and Amazon. 70% struggled with filters; 50% found checkout distracting.
  • User Personas: Sarah (27), loves browsing seasonal deals; Tom (35), needs a quick checkout for gadgets.
  • User Flow: Home > Categories > Product > Cart > Payment > Confirmation.
  • Wireframes: Homepage with search and categories, product listing with filtering, and sticky cart icon.
  • Visual Design: Modern white/navy layout, large product cards, and strong CTAs like "Add to Cart" and "Buy Now."
  • Prototype: Created a responsive Figma prototype with smooth transitions and easy cart access.

Challenges

Ensuring users have both exploratory browsing options and quick-purchase functionality for urgent buyers.

Key Learnings

A streamlined product discovery process paired with clear CTAs can significantly boost conversion rates. Users appreciated simplified checkout screens with fewer distractions.
